Josephus Problem


Story behind Flavius Josephus

Flavius Josephus was a Jewish historian and leader who lived in the first century. According to Josephus's firsthand account of the siege of Yodfat, he and his 40 soldiers were trapped in a cave by Roman soldiers. They chose suicide over capture, and settled on a serial method of committing suicide by drawing lots. Josephus states that by luck or possibly by the hand of God, he and another man remained until the end and surrendered to the Romans rather than killing themselves.

But the surviving Slavonic manuscript of Josephus tell a different story: that he “counted the numbers cunningly and so managed to deceive all the others”.

Centuries later, we try to put ourselves in the shoes of Josephus, and try to solve his problem:
  • There are n individuals, arranged in a circle, facing the centre and marked from 1 to n.
  • Starting from the 1st person, we follow a counting out process, in which each person kills the kth person on his left.
  • Note that there will be only one survivor on the end.
    We intend to find that position beforehand (given the values of n and k) so that we can place ourselves on it!
